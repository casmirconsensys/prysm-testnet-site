Become a Mainnet Validator

Ethereum 2.0 is a public blockchain which implements the Ethereum 2.0 Phase 0 [specification](https://github.com/ethereum/eth2.0-specs) for a proof-of-stake network, enabling anyone holding ETH to join and participate. You can join by directly participating in the official Ethereum 2.0 launchpad. Our team, Prysmatic Labs, created a client implementation for Ethereum 2.0 written in Go, called Prysm.

The only and official Validator Deposit Contract is 0x00000000219ab540356cbb839cbe05303d7705fa. Do not send funds to any other contract, and do not send ETH directly to the contract. If you want to participate in eth2, use the eth2 launchpad launchpad.ethereum.org. Prysmatic Labs endorses this address as the recommended way to become an eth2 validator.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Prysm?

Built in Go and fully-open source, the network runs on an eth2 node implementation called Prysm. To participate in the network, anyone can send {{DEPOSIT_AMOUNT}} ETH from ETH1.0 into the official Validator Deposit Contract 0x00000000219ab540356cbb839cbe05303d7705fa, which will queue in the user as a validator in the system. Validators participate in proposing and voting on blocks in the protocol, and the network applies rewards/penalties based on their behavior.

Why should I care?

Ethereum 2.0 is a massive, multi-year effort to create a true world computer. In other words, its goal is to create a truly decentralized, permissionless platform for programmable money through smart contracts. Phase 0, which Prysm implements, is a great step forward in making the years of research behind the future of Ethereum a tangible reality.
